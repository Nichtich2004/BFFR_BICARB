Why Bicarb Works

During intense exercise, hydrogen ions (H⁺) accumulate in muscles, causing acidosis — the burning sensation that limits performance. Sodium bicarbonate (NaHCO₃) acts as an extracellular buffer, neutralizing H⁺ ions and extending high-intensity capacity. Research confirms 2–3% performance gains in efforts lasting 30 seconds to 12 minutes.
